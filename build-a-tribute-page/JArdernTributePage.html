Jacinda Ardern

Prime Minister of Aotearoa New Zealand | Advocate for her people and planet

Notable Achievements

  • In 2017 Jacinda Kate Laurell Ardern became New Zealand's 40th Prime Minister, saying her goverment would be "focused, empathetic, and strong," and continued to serve a second term after the 2020 election.
  • Among other social efforts, Ardern's Families Package (2018) increased parental leave to 26 weeks and provided $60 per week to families with young children.
  • Internationally, Ardern has called for attention to the mistreatment of Muslims in China and Myanmar, effects and causes of climate change, and equality for women.
  • After the Christchurch mosque shootings in 2019, Ardern was praised for promoting unity and passion while tightening gun laws and working with tech companies to seek ways of eliminating terrorist and violent extremist content online.
  • Ardern was praised for her quick response and communication with the public during the Coronavirus pandemic starting in March 2020.
  • In June 2021, Adern and the New Zealand goverment formally aplogized for the Dawn Raids in the 1970s and 1980s that disproportionately impacted Pacifika people.
  • Adern delivered Harvard University's May 2022 commencement address, beginning the speech in Maori.
